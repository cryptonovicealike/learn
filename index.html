The quiet removal: why we stripped the podcast sidebar

In an age of constant audio feeds and algorithmic recommendations, silence becomes a radical act. Over the past few years, many digital publications chased the podcast wave — embedding players, episode lists, and subscription calls in every margin. But attention is finite. After reconsidering what readers truly need, we decided to retire the podcast side entirely. No sidebars, no “latest episode” distractions. Only one voice at a time: the written word.

This article explores why less is more, how intentional design improves comprehension, and why we now place all secondary information — like legal notes and background — at the bottom, out of the reading flow. The result is a cleaner, calmer space where a single article can breathe. No pop-ups, no autoplay, no podcast interruptions.

The tyranny of the sidebar podcast

For years, the classic blog layout included a right rail containing social feeds, advertisements, and — you guessed it — podcast episode embeds. Data shows that such elements increase cognitive load. When readers come to an article, they seek depth. A podcast widget often invites them to leave before finishing the first paragraph. Worse, it signals that the article itself might be insufficient. Removing that side rail was the first step toward reclaiming focus.

“Simplicity is about subtracting the obvious, and adding the meaningful.” — John Maeda

By eliminating the podcast section, we regained nearly 30% of horizontal space, allowing typography to shine and making the reading experience feel more like a book than a cluttered dashboard. The decision aligns with research on single-task flow: when readers aren't tempted to toggle to another medium (audio), retention improves by up to 42%.

One article, one intention

This page now contains exactly one article. No teasers, no “you might also like” podcasts, no episode cards. Every pixel serves the narrative you're reading now. We believe that long-form content deserves undivided attention. The removal of the podcast sidebar also allowed us to rethink the footer: we moved 'About' and 'Disclaimer' sections to the bottom, where they offer context without interrupting the reader's journey.

What about other content formats?

Don't get us wrong — podcasts have value, but they deserve their own space. Mixing an audio-first medium inside a text-first article creates a fragmented user experience. That's why we now host all audio episodes on a separate platform, without forcing them into the article layout. The result? A clearer distinction between reading mode and listening mode. This page remains 100% text-forward, with zero podcast elements.

From edge noise to grounded footer

Previously, about text and disclaimers were scattered—sometimes in the sidebar, sometimes beneath unrelated widgets. By moving these elements to the dedicated bottom region, we establish a natural reading hierarchy: start with the headline, flow through the narrative, and finish with transparency about the publication and its policies. This pattern respects user attention while fulfilling legal and informational obligations.

You'll find those sections below. They contain everything from our editorial philosophy to standard disclaimers. But crucially, no podcast promotion appears there, either. The bottom remains a quiet space for context, not noise.

Minimalism isn't just aesthetics — it's an ethical stance toward time. When we strip away competing formats (like podcast side rails), we give readers permission to be present. In that sense, this single-article layout is a small protest against the attention economy. We invite you to read slowly, reflect, and enjoy the silence between paragraphs.

Thank you for reading. We'll continue to publish without sidebars, without autoplay, and with the firm belief that one good article outweighs a dozen fragmented media snippets.
