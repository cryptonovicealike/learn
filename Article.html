What is Bitcoin? A Beginner's Guide

🎧 Listen to the Podcast: “Bitcoin Audible” – Episode 1

Imagine a form of money that isn't controlled by any bank or government. A currency you can send to anyone, anywhere, instantly, without a middleman. That’s Bitcoin.

The Basics

Bitcoin is the world’s first decentralized digital currency. It was created in 2009 by an anonymous person (or group) using the name Satoshi Nakamoto. There are no physical coins – it exists purely online, secured by a technology called blockchain.

How It Works (Simplified)

  • Decentralized network: Thousands of computers around the world verify transactions.
  • Blockchain: A public ledger that records every transaction ever made.
  • Limited supply: Only 21 million Bitcoins will ever exist.

💡 Key Insight: Bitcoin’s value comes from scarcity, security, and the fact that no single entity controls it.

Why People Use Bitcoin

  • Store of value: Often called “digital gold” because of its fixed supply.
  • Fast international transfers: Send value across borders in minutes.
  • Financial sovereignty: You control your money without needing a bank.

Getting Started

If you’re curious, start with a small amount. Learn how to set up a wallet (like BlueWallet or Trust Wallet) and buy a tiny fraction of a Bitcoin (you can buy as little as $10 worth). The journey is about understanding, not gambling.
